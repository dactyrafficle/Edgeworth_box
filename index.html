Each person forms its own demand function. The sum equals market demand. Market supply is the total market endowment. Solving it gives us price and thus the budget line.

These 2 make trades along the budget line. At a given point, mu.x/px reports the amount of utility a person can buy for $1.

If mu.x/px > mu.y/px, it means that $1 spent on x will provide more utility than $1 spent on y.

Such a person will probably want to sell k units of y, receive k·py money and purchase k·py/px units of x.

The trade cost is k·mu.y utility, but the gain is k·(py/px)·mu.x utility.

The contract curve is where amy.mrs = mark.mrs

MU/price is the real driver of change. Obviously the MRSs are equal at equilibrium, but thats just a final result. while MU/prices are not equal, that impacts the market, and drives a change in price
